2. Program Description

The Program delivers operational alerts and incident notifications to enrolled recipients. Messages cover the following five categories only:

  1. UPS power and battery alerts from monitored client sites
  2. Network device and switch outage alerts
  3. Microsoft Teams Phone and SBC service incidents
  4. Data-center facility alarms (temperature, power, environmental)
  5. Scheduled maintenance window confirmations

Messages contain only the alert type, the affected site, severity level, and a secure incident link for response coordination. No marketing, promotional, or third-party content is sent through this Program.

3. Message Frequency

Up to 30 messages per month. Actual frequency varies by on-call rotation activity and incident volume. Critical incident periods may briefly exceed the typical range.

4. Message and Data Rates

Msg & data rates may apply. Standard message and data rates from your wireless carrier may apply to each message sent or received under this Program. RingOn does not charge a separate fee for participation.

5. How to Opt In

You may enroll in the Program by:

  • Submitting the web opt-in form at https://ringonservice.net/sms-optin using your personal mobile number and explicit consent checkboxes; or
  • Submitting a signed paper enrollment form to RingOn HR or your team lead, indicating your personal mobile number and explicit written consent.

Upon enrollment, you will receive a confirmation message:

"RingOn Service Corp.: You are enrolled in operational SMS alerts. Reply STOP to opt out, HELP for help. Msg & data rates may apply. Up to 30 msgs/month."

6. How to Opt Out — Reply STOP

You may opt out of the Program at any time and free of charge by any of the following:

  • Replying STOP (or END, QUIT, CANCEL, UNSUBSCRIBE) to any Program SMS message
  • Visiting https://ringonservice.net/sms-optout
  • Emailing support@ringonservice.net

Opt-outs are processed automatically and immediately. You will receive a single confirmation reply, and no further Program messages will be sent. After opting out, you will continue to receive operational alerts via email and the internal incident dashboard.

Each Program message will identify RingOn as the sender, describe the alert type, the affected site, the severity level, and may include a secure URL link to the incident detail page on ringonservice.net for acknowledgement and coordination. Every message includes the STOP and HELP instructions. Messages do not contain:
