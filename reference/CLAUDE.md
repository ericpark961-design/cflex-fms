## Ground truth — do not regress

- The 6 SVGs in `icons/` were traced from the real product photos in `reference/`.
  Before changing any icon, open the matching `reference/<name>_real.png` and match it.
  Do not invent details. If unsure what a unit looks like, ask for a photo — never guess.
- Icons are generated by `scripts/gen_icons.py`. Edit the generator, then regenerate;
  do not hand-edit the SVG output (it will be overwritten).
- `fleet.json` is derived from the customer spreadsheet and maps all 44 real devices
  (HMA / KUS / HAEA) to an icon. Keep `iconForModel()` in `src/ups-models.ts` in sync
  with the model strings actually present there.

## Conventions

- Status palette and screen tints live in `src/status.ts` and mirror EcoStruxure IT
  severity colors. Reuse them everywhere; do not introduce new status colors.
- Every SVG exposes `id="apc-logo"` (swappable wordmark group) and `id="lcd-screen"`
  (status-tintable display). Preserve these ids when editing the generator.
- When inlining SVGs in the DOM, namespace ids per instance (see `InlineSvg` in
  `UpsIcon.tsx`) — the SVGs share ids and clipPath refs, so multiple raw inlines on one
  page WILL collide otherwise.
- The APC logo is a typographic approximation. If/when the official APC brand-kit SVG is
  provided, replace the `<g id="apc-logo">` contents only.

## Gotchas

- `cairosvg` is only for preview PNGs during generation; production just serves the SVGs.
- Symmetra is a floor tower (portrait aspect 300×440); the others are landscape rack units.
  `UPS_MODELS[*].aspect` already encodes this — use it for layout, don't hardcode sizes.
- srt5000.svg intentionally includes the SRT192RMBP battery pack stacked on top; if a
  bare-UPS variant is needed, add `srt5000_nobatt` in the generator rather than editing.
